A Closer Look at Bitcoin's Power Law and What the Next Cycle Might Tell Us

Originally shared by @toospooky on X · retrieved 2026-07-22. This is added context and credit, not the original work.

@toospooky posted a two-part Bitcoin analysis that I found myself reading twice. It is not a typical price prediction. It is a structural argument, and those tend to be worth sitting with.

What was bookmarked

@toospooky made a case built on the Bitcoin power law, a framework that tracks price against time on log-log scales, where the relationship has held roughly linear across more than a decade of cycles (as the framing goes). The argument: under that model, the next cycle bottom should land around $188k. For Bitcoin to maintain its trend through a normal -50% drawdown, the peak would need to reach the upper $300ks. The trend line for a 4-year-cycle bear puts the floor even higher. In a related post that this one quotes, @toospooky put it plainly: if Bitcoin is not well above $220k by fall of 2029, the move is to sell to stablecoin.

Both posts are by the same author. This is one person working through the math in public and putting a specific, testable threshold on the table.

A floor and a test. Simple enough to put on a calendar.

Why it matters

The power law frame changes the question. Instead of asking where Bitcoin goes next month, it asks whether a decade-long structural trend survives the next cycle. That shifts the focus away from short-term sentiment and back toward structure, which is a more durable place to start.

For anyone thinking about crypto in longer time horizons, that reframe matters. Builders managing treasury exposure, long-hold participants, and people trying to filter signal from noise in Bitcoin discussion all benefit from having something specific to track rather than an opinion to argue with. A framework that can break, and tells you when, is more useful than a confident call that refuses to be falsified.

It is also one of the rare Bitcoin analysis posts that leads with what the trend says rather than what someone hopes it will say.

The practical breakdown

What the argument says. The Bitcoin power law, as the framing goes, describes a log-log relationship between price and time that has held across multiple cycles. The claim from @toospooky is that this model puts the next cycle bottom at roughly $188k, and that reaching the upper $300ks as a peak is what the trend requires to survive a standard drawdown intact.

The $220k threshold. The quoted post is the more usable version of the thesis. "Well above $220k by fall 2029" is specific and binary. It either happens or it does not. That kind of concrete marker tells you when to reassess, which is more practical than holding a model that never gives you a clear out.

Who it helps. Anyone thinking about crypto allocation across multi-year windows. This is not for short-term traders. It is for long-horizon participants and builders who want a structural reference point rather than market sentiment to navigate by.

What still needs work. Historical trends can break. The power law has held for over a decade, which is meaningful data, but it is still a model fitted to past behavior. The specific numbers, $188k, upper $300ks, and $220k, all come from fitting that model to history. Worth watching rather than anchoring to.

What to track. The $220k by 2029 threshold is the right thing to put on a calendar. If the market clears that level with room to spare, the power law is probably intact. If it falls short or barely touches it, the structural argument gets more complicated.

Rick's take

What I appreciate about this kind of post is that it gives you something concrete to hold onto when the noise gets loud. Putting your own exit condition in writing, $220k or stablecoins by fall 2029, takes a certain clarity. Whether the prediction lands or not, the framework is useful because it tells you exactly when the model breaks. That is genuinely more valuable than a confident call that leaves you no clear moment to reassess.

I am not in the business of calling Bitcoin cycles, but this is a reference point worth revisiting in a couple of years.

What to watch next

The $220k by fall 2029 marker from @toospooky is the cleanest version of this thesis to track. If Bitcoin clears it comfortably, the power law argument looks durable. If it falls short, the 4-year-cycle model and its floor assumptions will need rethinking. Worth checking back against this framework around 2027 to see how the trajectory is shaping up.
