Why ChiaPhunks Rewarding Its BEPE Liquidity Providers Caught My Attention

Originally shared by @bytesandblocks on X · retrieved 2026-07-16. This is added context and credit, not the original work.

I stopped on this one because it is a small, quiet example of a community actually rewarding the people doing unglamorous work. No big announcement, no thread explaining tokenomics, just a shoutout post. That is usually where the real signal is.

What was bookmarked

@bytesandblocks posted that ChiaPhunks airdropped NFTs to the top nine places in the $BEPE liquidity pool. The post congratulates the group that made the list, including @sir_stake_a_lot, @Fanatikvibes, @theguywshades, @Ashen_R0, @CassXCH, @GeneralDeeez, @ouchmykryptoe, and @SpeechlessZi. It calls out @SpeechlessZi and @theguywshades specifically for adding a meaningful amount of liquidity since the previous week. There is an image attached to the post but no external links, no docs, and no numbers on pool size or airdrop value.

The basic loop: provide liquidity, get recognized for it.

Why it matters

Liquidity is the boring infrastructure underneath every token that people actually want to trade. Nobody tweets about providing LP the way they tweet about a price chart, but without people willing to lock up capital in a pool, a token like $BEPE does not have real depth to trade against. Rewarding the top liquidity providers with an NFT airdrop is a direct way to say: the people keeping this market functional matter, and here is something to show for it.

This fits into a pattern I keep seeing across the Chia ecosystem: smaller projects using NFTs and direct shoutouts instead of big marketing budgets to build loyalty. It is community building through recognition, not spectacle.

The practical breakdown

What it does: ChiaPhunks used its NFT collection as a reward mechanism for the people providing the most liquidity to the $BEPE pool, ranking the top nine spots.

Who it helps: The liquidity providers get a tangible reward beyond whatever trading fees the pool generates, and the token itself benefits from deeper, stickier liquidity because there is now a reason to keep adding to the pool instead of just parking capital elsewhere.

Why builders should care: This is a low cost, low complexity way to incentivize a specific behavior (liquidity provision) using an asset the project already controls (its NFT collection). No new token needed, no complicated vesting schedule, just direct recognition tied to a snapshot of pool activity.

What still needs work: The post does not say how the rankings are tracked, how often this happens, or whether it is a one time thing or a recurring incentive. Without a public dashboard, participants are trusting the project's own numbers, which is common in small ecosystems but worth someone building a simple public tracker for eventually.

What I would test first: If I were running something similar, I would want a public, verifiable snapshot of LP positions before the airdrop, just so the recognition carries a bit more weight and other holders can see the leaderboard for themselves.

Rick's take

I like seeing this kind of thing pop up in the Chia corner of the timeline. It is a small move, but it is the right instinct: find the people quietly doing the work that keeps a token tradeable, and make sure they know it did not go unnoticed. @SpeechlessZi and @theguywshades adding real liquidity since last week is exactly the kind of behavior that keeps a young pool from drying up, and calling that out by name matters more than people give it credit for. The one thing I would love to see next is a public way to check the leaderboard, just so the whole community can watch it happen in real time instead of taking the announcement on faith.

What to watch next

Worth keeping an eye on whether ChiaPhunks turns this into a recurring reward cycle, and whether other Chia projects start copying the pattern of using existing NFT collections as liquidity incentives instead of minting new reward tokens. If a public LP tracker shows up alongside it, that would be a real step toward making this kind of program self-verifying.
