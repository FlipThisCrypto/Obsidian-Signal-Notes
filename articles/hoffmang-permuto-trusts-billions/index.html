What Gene Hoffman's Permuto Trusts comment might actually mean

Originally shared by @hoffmang on X · retrieved 2025-03-21. This is added context and credit, not the original work.

I stopped on this one because of who said it, not because of what it proved. Gene Hoffman has been around Chia long enough that when he says something with this much confidence, it is worth at least writing down and watching, even without the receipts yet.

What was bookmarked

@hoffmang posted a short, casual reply telling an anon that they were going to bring billions of dollars into the Permuto Trusts this summer, and tagged @permutocapital in the post. That is the whole claim. No numbers beyond "billions," no timeline beyond "this summer," no links to a deck, filing, or announcement. It reads like an inside joke crossed with a genuine prediction, the kind of tweet that is more teaser than proof.

The idea in one sketch: traditional trust money potentially finding its way onto blockchain rails.

Why it matters

Permuto Capital has been building trust structures on top of Chia, aimed at bringing traditional finance style products, think custody and trust accounts, onto a blockchain rail that was designed from the start with regulators and long term custody in mind. That is the whole pitch behind Chia's architecture: proof of space and time, clawback and custody features, and a chain built to be boring and safe rather than flashy. If real institutional money starts moving into structures like this, it is a meaningful test of whether that design philosophy actually attracts serious capital, not just enthusiasts.

Hoffman is not a random account. He has been a public face of Chia Network for years, so his read on where trust money is headed carries more weight than a typical anon's. That does not make the number true. It makes it worth paying attention to.

The practical breakdown

What it points at: Institutional or high net worth capital potentially flowing into Permuto's trust products, which are built on Chia rails.

Who it helps if true: The broader Chia ecosystem, since real trust and custody volume is exactly the use case Chia was designed for, plus anyone building adjacent tooling, wallets, or reporting infrastructure for that kind of money.

What is still missing: Any actual numbers, filings, or confirmations. "Billions this summer" is a prediction from one person on X, not an announcement. There is no linked press release, no SEC filing, no on chain data cited in the post itself.

What I would watch for: Any follow up from @permutocapital with specifics, any on chain activity that lines up with the claim, and whether "this summer" actually produces something concrete or quietly slides.

Rick's take

I like seeing this kind of confidence out of someone who has been building in Chia long enough to have earned an opinion. Gene has watched this ecosystem from the early days, and when he says something like this, my instinct is to take it as a genuine signal of what he is seeing internally, not just hype for hype's sake. Permuto going after real trust and custody money is exactly the kind of use case Chia was built for, and if even a fraction of "billions" shows up, that is a real milestone for the chain.

The one thing I will say plainly: this is a tweet, not a term sheet. I want to see it happen before I count it. But I would rather be the guy who was cautiously excited early than the guy who dismissed it and missed the story.

What to watch next

Keep an eye on @permutocapital's own posts and any public filings or partnership announcements over the next few months. If real capital starts moving, there should be traces of it, wallet activity, trust filings, or at minimum a more detailed follow up from the people involved. If summer comes and goes with nothing more specific than this tweet, that tells its own story too.
