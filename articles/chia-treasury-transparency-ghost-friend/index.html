What was bookmarked

The breakdown: 331 XCH paired into liquidity for a token called $BITCOIN, 198 XCH sent to @mojoshi_xch, and 33 XCH each earmarked next for "tang gang" and a "wizard treasury." There is an image attached to the original post, and no external links beyond that. This reads like an internal ledger update shared publicly for the sake of transparency.

One treasury, four destinations: liquidity, a transfer, and two community allocations.

Why it matters

Chia has always had this pocket of community-built tokens and treasuries running alongside the more infrastructure-focused side of the ecosystem, and a lot of it lives and dies on trust. When someone posts the actual XCH amounts moving between a liquidity pool, a wallet, and community treasuries, that is a small but real act of accountability. It is the same instinct behind dashboards and on-chain explorers: show the numbers, let people check the math themselves.

It also says something about how these community treasuries function day to day. They are not abstract concepts, they are actual XCH balances being split and moved on a schedule, with someone willing to post the breakdown instead of just saying "funded" and moving on.

The practical breakdown

What it shows: a real-time snapshot of treasury flow, split across liquidity provisioning, a direct transfer, and two community allocations.

Who it helps: anyone holding or watching $BITCOIN, anyone in tang gang or connected to the wizard treasury, and honestly anyone who wants a small example of what transparent community fund management can look like on Chia.

What is still unclear: the post does not link a block explorer transaction or a dashboard, so there is no easy way for an outside reader to verify the numbers directly from the post alone. That does not mean it is wrong, it just means the verification step lives outside this specific post.

What I would want next: a public, ongoing ledger or dashboard link attached to updates like this, so the community does not have to rely on someone remembering to post a thread every time funds move.

Rick's take

I like seeing this kind of post. What to watch next

Worth watching whether $BITCOIN's liquidity keeps growing, whether tang gang and the wizard treasury start showing visible output from these allocations, and whether this kind of public ledger update becomes a habit rather than a one-off. None of that is guaranteed, but the willingness to post real numbers is a decent early signal.
