Rough notes, not a scoreboard: speed, cost, quality, and question marks.

Why it matters

There is a lot of noise right now about which model is best, and most of it comes from people who ran one prompt and declared a winner. I do not think that is useful. What is useful is someone who is actually switching between tools mid-project, for real tasks, and can say plainly where each one shines and where it does not. That is what I was trying to do here, even in a short post.

This also matters because most people building things right now are not loyal to one model. They are picking whichever tool fits the job that day. Being honest about strengths and weaknesses, instead of picking a favorite and defending it, is more useful to anyone trying to decide where to spend their time or their credits.

The practical breakdown

What each tool is doing for me right now:

Grok 4.5 is earning a spot in my rotation because it is fast and it has been pleasantly surprising, especially since I came into it with low expectations as a newer user. Speed matters more than people give it credit for when you are iterating quickly.

Codex Luna 4.6 is the one I am withholding judgment on. I do not want to hand out a verdict before I have used it enough to trust my own read on it. That is not a knock, it is just honesty about where I am in the process.

Claude Code Fable 5.0 is the one that costs me the most in credits, and I noticed that immediately. But the output quality has been strong enough that I keep reaching for it anyway when the task actually needs that level of care.

Gemini is the one I have the most mixed feelings about. There are a few areas where it falls short for what I need, but it also has some clear benefits, which is why I still keep it in the mix rather than write it off.

Who this helps: anyone juggling multiple AI tools for real work and trying to figure out which one to reach for on a given day, instead of picking one tool and forcing every task through it.

What still needs work: my own read on Codex Luna is not settled yet, and I want more time with Gemini before I say anything sharper than "a few areas are lacking."

Rick's take

What I like about this week is that none of these tools felt interchangeable. Each one has a personality, and once you notice that, you start routing work to the right one instead of forcing everything through a single model out of habit. Grok surprising me as a newer user is the part I am most excited about, mostly because I was not expecting to like it this much this fast. Claude Code Fable earning its credit cost is a good sign too, since that usually means the output is actually saving me time somewhere else. Gemini is the one I want to keep an open mind about. A tool having a few weak spots does not mean it is not worth keeping around, especially if the benefits show up in the right situations.

What to watch next

I am going to keep using Codex Luna 4.6 until I have a real opinion instead of a placeholder one. I also want to spend more time figuring out exactly where Gemini's benefits show up best for me, since "a few areas lacking" is not the same as "not useful." If anything changes meaningfully with any of these four, I will post an update rather than let a first impression sit unchallenged.
