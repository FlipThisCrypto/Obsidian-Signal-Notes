Different models, different dials. Guardrails are a design choice, not a fixed constant.

Why it matters

This touches a theme that keeps coming up as more capable open and semi-open models show up outside the usual US labs: the gap between what a model can technically do and what it is allowed to do by design. Guardrails and copyright filtering are not neutral technical details. They are product decisions, and different labs are clearly making different calls. When a model loosens those calls, some people experience that as more useful, some see it as reckless, and most of the reaction depends on what you were trying to do with the model in the first place.

For builders, this matters because model choice is turning into a values choice as much as a capability choice. Two models can be equally good at writing a React component and completely different in how they handle a request that brushes up against copyrighted material or a sensitive prompt. That difference is becoming a real part of the decision tree, not a footnote.
