Why it matters

In-person meetups in a niche crypto ecosystem are not automatically significant. But they are a different kind of signal than social media activity. A meetup means people made plans, arranged travel or time off, and showed up in the same room because they decided it was worth the effort. That kind of commitment is harder to manufacture than engagement metrics.

As the Chia project describes its infrastructure, the network is built on proof-of-space-and-time consensus and includes tools like DataLayer, decentralized identifiers, and CATs, among other components. The developer ecosystem has kept building through multiple market cycles. That kind of quiet persistence tends to surface in community events before it surfaces anywhere else.

The frame @Alfonsojm1 puts around the Toronto announcement is also worth reading carefully. He is not promising a price target. He is making a structural observation: this kind of long-horizon bet has paid off before for people who stayed in, and the right posture is to keep moving on what you can currently see rather than waiting until the whole future is visible. That is a practical description of how early-stage networks have tended to work, not a hype claim.
