Why it matters

The perception gap is the actual problem.

Chia has a real smart contract layer. ChiaLisp is a real language. The coin-set model the chain runs on is a genuinely interesting approach to programmable money, distinct from both Ethereum's account model and Bitcoin's raw UTXO approach. The tooling has matured. Projects have shipped on-chain.

The persistent framing in some corners of crypto still treats Chia as primarily a storage play. That was never the complete picture, and it is even less accurate now. Builders who dismiss Chia without looking at the development layer are skipping a chapter that is worth reading.

The practical breakdown

ChiaLisp and CLVM. The smart contract language on Chia is ChiaLisp, a Lisp dialect that compiles to the Chia Lisp Virtual Machine (CLVM). It is different from Solidity in meaningful ways. The learning curve is real. But the security model is interesting: contracts are stateless puzzle-and-solution pairs rather than mutable state machines, which changes how you think about what can go wrong at the contract level.

The coin-set model. Instead of accounts, Chia uses coins. Each coin is locked by a puzzle (the contract logic) and unlocked by a solution (the spending conditions). Coins are immutable once created. This means there is no shared mutable state to corrupt across transactions. Some categories of exploits common on account-based chains simply do not apply in the same way here.

What you can build. CATs (Chia Asset Tokens) let you issue fungible tokens. NFTs are supported and have their own standard on the chain. Decentralized identity (DIDs) is built into the base layer. The Offers protocol enables atomic swaps directly in the wallet, without needing a separate DEX contract. Custom smart coins can handle multi-signature logic, escrow, automated treasury functions, and more.

Tooling and documentation. The Chia developer docs exist and are actively maintained. The CDV (Chia Dev Tools) CLI gives you a local testing environment. The community has been building libraries and utilities for years. It is not Ethereum in terms of ecosystem size, but it is not a blank page either. The infrastructure for serious builder work is there.

The ecosystem is smaller than Ethereum, but that also means your project is more visible and the community is easier to reach. There are real people here working on real things.
