The Part of Chia That Has Nothing to Do With Code

Originally shared by @yakuhito on X · retrieved 2026-07-14. This is added context and credit, not the original work.

I bookmarked this one because it is a good reminder that not everything worth saying about a crypto ecosystem is about the tech. Sometimes the best part is just the people.

What was bookmarked

@yakuhito posted a short, simple thought: one thing he appreciates about Chia has nothing to do with the technology. It is the people. He talked about meeting amazing people through Chia, both online and at in-person meetups, and said he always leaves those meetups feeling inspired. That is the whole post. No thread, no announcement, no pitch. Just a thank you to the community.

Online conversation and in-person meetups reinforcing each other, the two halves of a community that actually holds together.

Why it matters

It is easy to talk about blockchains purely in terms of consensus mechanisms, tokenomics, or roadmaps. Chia gets plenty of that kind of coverage, and it deserves it, the tech is genuinely different from most of the space. But ecosystems do not survive on tech alone. They survive because people show up, keep building, keep showing each other their work, and keep coming back to meetups even after the hype cycles come and go.

@yakuhito is one of the more active builders in the Chia ecosystem, and posts like this matter because they say something a whitepaper cannot: that the human layer is working. People are meeting up. People are inspiring each other. That is not a small thing in a space that burns a lot of people out.

The practical breakdown

What it points to: a functioning, in-person and online community around Chia, not just a group of wallets holding a token.

Who it helps: newer builders or curious people trying to figure out if a project has real humans behind it or just marketing. Posts like this are a signal, not proof, but a signal worth noticing.

What problem it touches on: crypto communities often live entirely on timelines and Discord servers. The ones that also gather in person tend to have more staying power, because relationships built face to face are harder to walk away from than a group chat.

What I would test first: if you are curious about Chia and have never been to a meetup, that is the actual test here, not reading more threads. Go to one, see if the energy matches what gets described online.

Rick's take

This is the kind of post I like bookmarking even though there is nothing to build or analyze. I have been around Chia long enough to know this feeling is real and not exaggerated for engagement. The meetups are genuinely good. You leave them with more energy than you walked in with, and that is rare in any space, crypto or otherwise.

What I like about @yakuhito saying this specifically is that he is one of the people actually shipping tools for the ecosystem, so it is not coming from someone with nothing else to point to. It is coming from someone deep in the technical side who is choosing to highlight the human side instead. That says something about where his priorities are, and I respect that.

What to watch next

Nothing here is a prediction or a roadmap item, it is just a mood check on the community. Worth watching whether more builders start posting this kind of thing, and whether local Chia meetups keep growing. A healthy technical ecosystem and a healthy human one tend to move together, so this is as good a signal as any to keep an eye on.
