The Sinking Ship: P7 Fulfillment Core and a Fairness Reveal Site Just Landed

Originally shared by on X · retrieved 2026-07-17. This is added context and credit, not the original work.

The Sinking Ship is a project I've been building in the open on GitHub. It's still early, and I'm not going to dress it up as more than it is right now, but the last 24 hours moved it forward in a real way: the fulfillment core for what I'm calling P7 is in, along with a fairness and reveal site, and I cleared a stack of open questions that had been hanging over the build.

What just shipped

P7 fulfillment core. This is the backend piece that actually handles fulfilling whatever the system promises, the part that turns a commitment into a completed action. Getting this in place means the project has a working spine instead of just scaffolding.

Fairness and reveal site. Alongside the core, I stood up a site dedicated to fairness and reveals. This is the transparency layer, the place where whatever needs to be shown or verified gets shown or verified, separate from the main mechanics. Having this live as its own surface is a deliberate choice: fairness shouldn't be buried inside the main app, it should be easy to point at.

Resolved open questions. I also closed out a batch of OQs (open questions) that had been sitting unresolved. These are the kinds of decisions that quietly block progress until somebody actually sits down and answers them. Getting through them clears the runway for the next stretch of work.

Fulfillment core on one side, fairness and reveal on the other.

Why it matters

A fulfillment core is the difference between a project that talks about doing something and one that actually does it. Pairing that with a dedicated fairness/reveal site means the trust layer isn't an afterthought, it's built alongside the mechanics from the start. And resolving open questions early, rather than letting them pile up, is the kind of unglamorous work that keeps a solo or small-team project from stalling out later.

Rick's take

I like shipping updates like this one because it's not a flashy feature, it's infrastructure. The fulfillment core is the part of The Sinking Ship that actually has to work every single time, no exceptions, and getting it live is the milestone that lets everything else stack on top of it. The fairness/reveal site is the part I'm genuinely excited about, because I think transparency works better when it lives somewhere people can point to directly instead of trusting a claim buried in the app. Clearing the open questions was mostly just discipline, sit down, make the calls, move on, but it's the kind of progress that doesn't show up in a screenshot and still matters just as much as the code.

What's next

With the fulfillment core and the fairness/reveal site both live, the next stretch is about building on that foundation and stress-testing it before layering in more. I'll keep posting updates here as the commits land.
